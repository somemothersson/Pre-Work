Gram Parson's - Grevious Angel

Too Country for LA, Too Rock n' Roll for Nashville

Gram Parsons' refered to his style Country, Rock n' Roll, and R&B as Cosmic American Music. Most well known for his flamboyant Nudie suit, seen above. Adorned with pot leaves, poppies, and pills. It served as foreshadowing to his pre-mature death from a heroin overdose, followed by the failed attempt of his manager to burn his stolen body in Joshua Tree National Park.

Gram Left his mark behind in numerous records. In addtion to his two solo Albums, GP and Grevious Angel. Gram also played as member and leader of a number of bands.

    Such As:
  • The International Submarine Band
  • The Byrds
  • The Flying Burrito Brothers

Gram also launched the career of singer Emmylou Harris, inviting her to perform on GP and Grevious Angel. Rolling Stone recently wrote an article with 10 interesting facts
